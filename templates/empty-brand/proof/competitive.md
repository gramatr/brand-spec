## How we position

We compete on **time-to-value and integration depth**, not on feature
breadth. When a buyer asks "why you instead of {Competitor A}," the
honest one-line answer is: *they're a platform, we're a workflow that
ships in a week.*

## Competitor map

### Competitor A — the incumbent platform

- **Where they win:** enterprise procurement, breadth of modules,
  brand recognition.
- **Where we win:** speed of deployment, opinionated defaults, lower
  total cost for teams under 500 seats.
- **Honest weakness on our side:** we don't have their reporting depth.
  Don't pretend we do.

### Competitor B — the cheaper alternative

- **Where they win:** sticker price, self-serve sign-up.
- **Where we win:** support, integration quality, retention.
- **Honest weakness on our side:** higher upfront cost. Lead with TCO,
  not list price.

### Competitor C — the adjacent player

- Not a direct competitor today, but watch them. Overlap likely in 2027
  if their roadmap holds.

## Rules of engagement

- Never name a competitor in external content unless the customer asks
  first.
- When asked, be specific and fair. No straw-manning.
- If we lose a deal, log the reason in CRM with the competitor field
  set so this file can be revised quarterly.
